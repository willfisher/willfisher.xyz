In this post we seek to prove the following theorem.

<div class="theorem">
	Let \(\theta\) be irrational. Define \(\rho_{\theta}:\mathbb{N}\to \mathbb{N}\) by
	\[\rho_{\theta}(x)=\sum_{n=1}^{x}(-1)^{\lfloor n\theta\rfloor},\]
	then \(\rho_{\theta}\) is unbounded.
</div>
